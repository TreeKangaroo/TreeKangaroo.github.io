Wearable Device for Gait Analysis

In 9th grade, I developed a wearable device to collect accelerometer and gyroscope data for gait analysis. I also wrote Python programs to track knee angles during walking based on the collected sensor data. After attending the Stanford AI4ALL summer program in 2020, I was inspired to use AI techniques to analyze wearable device data for running form classification. I love running and would like to use technology to help reduce running-related injuries. I presented the wearable device design and knee angle tracking method at the 2020 Illinois JSHS and won 3rd place. The AI-based running form classification work was presented at the 2021 Illinois JSHS and won 1st place. The two phases of the project were selected to participate in both 2020 and 2021 National JSHS (poster in 2020 and oral presentation in 2021).

Summer Science Program in Astrophysics

In the summer of 2022, I attended the Summer Science Program in Astrophysics at the University of Colorado Boulder. I performed astronomical observations and wrote original Python code (using the Method of Gauss) to determine the orbit of a near Earth asteroid (2005 XJ8). In some leisure time, I also tried my hand at astrophotography.
